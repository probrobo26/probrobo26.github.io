## Reflecting on Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
We spent the last several weeks dissecting a modern compositional framework for enabling a robot to autonomously act in a world under various forms of uncertainty. We're going to start class reflecting on the key concepts from this unit and their implications. 

With a small group, spend about 15-20 minutes summarizing and organizing key ideas from this unit. Some vocabulary to get you started (by no means exhaustive!):
* [Sequential] Decision Processes
* Uncertainty
* Belief
* Reward Approximation
* [Optimal] Policy
* Search
* MDP / POMDP
* Monte Carlo Sampling
* Value / Policy Iteration
* Information-Gathering
* Explore-Exploit

Once you've completed your maps, consider the following questions:
* How does understanding planning under uncertainty frameworks change or enhance your understanding of "intelligent" robots? 
* What should an engineer developing "intelligent" autonomous robots keep in mind when specifying a task and choosing a solver?
