## Concept Recap and Review
We've spent this semester learning about how we can enable robotic systems to grapple with _uncertainty_. Let's take a little bit of time to review some of the key concepts and relationships between them. 

To structure this concept mapping activity, let's go back to our original framing: State-Action-Sense. Consider the following in these categories:
* What type of uncertainty appears in each of these categories? How is it represented? How is it managed?
* What _tasks_ do we set robots to do? How does uncertainty impact their ability to carry out these tasks?
* Consider the structure of the generic Bayesian framework -- where do we see this framework applied?

## Defining Intelligence in Robotics
Increasingly, "intelligence" is a hallmark trait that technology is being designed to possess. While generative artificial intelligence systems based on large-language models have started to see wide-scale consumer and industry adoption, these systems are entirely software oriented. _Intelligent robots_ are embodied: they contain an artificial "brain" with the ability to make decisions and learn through the use of end effectors and sensors.

We have been building the foundational tools to make intelligent robots all semester, under the definition of "intelligence" that is inclusive of task-based decision-making and planning.

In small groups, pick one of the robots you initially investigated in the [very first assignment in this class (Problem 2)](./day01.md). For this robot, answer the following:
* For what types of tasks is this robot designed? Does the physical form of this robot limit it to these types of tasks?
* What types of uncertainty does this robot encounter (or could encounter)?
* What types of decisions does this robot make?
* What type of world models / state estimates do you think this robot attempts to learn to carry out its tasks? 
* What risks are associated with this robot's failure to perceive and estimate the world accurately? Is "retraining" the robot expensive or cheap?
* How would you rate the "intelligence" of this robot? Do you think this intelligence level is purposefully designed or is a limitation of current technology?

We will do a round-robin discussion of your conclusions at the end of the class!
